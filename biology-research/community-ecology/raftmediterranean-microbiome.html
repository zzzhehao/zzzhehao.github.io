I worked with Wolbachia in my bachelor thesis - everyone who knows them should be aware of their incredible evolution. I searched for Wolbachia out of curious in the dataset, and found an entry in Prok_taxadata.txt.

No comments on this since there’s no entry in quality-controlled ASV dataset. But after I read the paper (Parvathi et al. 2020) that found Wolbachia sp being most abundant in deep-sea sediment microbial community, I would literally not be surprised anymore if people found Wolbachia being most dominant in the aerosol as next. They are so ABUNDANT and they are EVERYWHERE.

By the way, our Wolbachia - Altica lythri project is aiming for another publication in the proximate future. Stay tuned. See Rohlfing et al. (2024) for the latest publication.

## Miscellaneous

I worked with *Wolbachia* in my bachelor thesis - everyone who knows them should be aware of their incredible evolution. I looked for them out of curious in the dataset, and found an entry in [`Prok_taxadata.txt`](https://github.com/InaMariaDeutschmann/GlobalNetworkMalaspinaHotmix/blob/main/00_Tables/Prok_taxdata.txt). In terms of network analysis, it will be extremely interesting to do a meta analysis on interaction between *Wolbachia* and their hosts, as well as those among their hosts and among themselves, considering the frequently occurring horizontal transfer (host switch) and gene recombination. Although this will probably be insanely complex since they are capable of so many things.

No comments on this one here since they are not included in quality-controlled ASV dataset, so it probably came from by-catch of their hosts or contamination. But after I read the paper [@parvathi2020] that found *Wolbachia sp.* being most abundant in deep-sea sediment microbial community, I would literally not be surprised anymore if people found *Wolbachia* being most dominant in the aerosol of high-sky as next. They are so ABUNDANT and they are EVERYWHERE.

By the way, our *Wolbachia* - *Altica lythri* project is aiming for another publication in the proximate future. Stay tuned. See @rohlfing2024 for the latest publication.
